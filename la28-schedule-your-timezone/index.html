About the LA 2028 Olympic Schedule

The Los Angeles 2028 Summer Olympics run from Friday, July 14 (Opening Ceremony) through Sunday, July 30, 2028 (Closing Ceremony). Competition in several sports begins two days early on Wednesday, July 12, with baseball, basketball, cricket, handball, hockey, and rugby sevens kicking off the Games.

This viewer uses the official LA28 Competition Schedule version 2.1, released February 6, 2025. All 772 sessions are included, spanning 36 core sports plus five new additions: baseball/softball, cricket, flag football, lacrosse, and squash. Source times are Pacific Time (PT), except softball and canoe slalom at the Oklahoma City venue, which use Central Time (CT).

How the timezone conversion works

Your browser's timezone is detected automatically when you open the page. You can also pick any major city from the dropdown to plan viewing times for friends, family, or for travel. Daylight Saving rules for your selected timezone are handled automatically — July 2028 falls during DST in most of the Northern Hemisphere.

Filtering by medal sessions

Turning on "Medals only" shows just the sessions where a gold or bronze medal is decided — 223 sessions in total. This is the shortlist for anyone who wants to watch finals without combing through preliminary rounds. Team finals, individual finals, and medal matches are all included.

What about the Opening and Closing Ceremonies?

Ceremony session codes and times are not yet finalized in the official schedule (as of v2.1). The Opening Ceremony is confirmed for July 14, 2028 at SoFi Stadium and the Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum (split-venue format for the first time in Olympic history). Closing Ceremony is July 30, 2028 at the LA Memorial Coliseum. This tool will be updated when official ceremony times are announced.

Football (Soccer) preliminary times

Men's and women's football preliminary matches (TBD venues outside LA) do not yet have confirmed times in schedule v2.1. These will populate automatically when LA28 releases updated data. Final stages at the Rose Bowl Stadium in Pasadena are already scheduled and visible in this viewer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this the official LA28 schedule? — No, this is an independent viewer built from the publicly released LA28 Competition Schedule v2.1. Always check la28.org for official ticketing and the latest updates.

How accurate are the times? — Times match the official schedule exactly. Timezone conversions use the IANA Time Zone Database (the same standard used by Google Calendar and every major OS).

Will the schedule change? — LA28 has stated the schedule is subject to change until the conclusion of the Games. This page will be updated whenever a new version is published.

Can I save events to my calendar? — Not yet — this is the MVP release. Google Calendar and iCal export are coming in the next update, along with the ability to save favorites and share filtered views.
